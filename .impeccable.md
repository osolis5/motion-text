# Project Design DNA — Generative Type Poster

## Identity
A projection-mapped generative typography poster (22" x 30") built with P5.js and Three.js. Not a screen — a physical light installation. Every decision must honor the fact that this lives on a wall, not in a browser.

## Aesthetic North Star
**Disciplined chaos.** Swiss structural rigor as a skeleton, punk energy as the flesh. The poster should feel like it was designed by someone who studied at Basel and then burned the curriculum. Think: Neville Brody's output colliding with a UV-lit warehouse rave.

## Brand Personality
- Confrontational, not aggressive
- Electric, not flashy
- Raw, not unfinished
- Monumental, not bloated

## Hard Rules
1. **No corporate minimalism.** No friendly corners, no approachable palettes, no SaaS energy.
2. **No static predictability.** Every frame must feel generative. No templates, no stock motion.
3. **Typography is the architecture.** Letters are not decoration — they ARE the structure.
4. **Black is sacred.** The void is not empty — it's the stage. Respect negative space.
5. **Projection-first.** Design for light hitting a surface, not pixels on glass. Contrast matters more than detail.

## Color Territory
Ultraviolet spectrum: deep indigo through violet to electric blue. Phosphorescent edge accents. Black void as foundation. No warm tones unless they're burning hot (white-hot accents only).

## Typography Territory
Ultra-bold display faces. Scale that bleeds off edges. Distortion, warping, stretching — legibility is secondary to impact. The letters should feel like they're trying to escape the surface.

## Technical Constraints
- Aspect ratio: 11:15 (22" x 30" portrait)
- Projection mapping via p5.mapper (p5.js 2.x)
- Three.js for 3D depth and lighting
- Must maintain 30+ FPS for smooth projection
- Calibration mode for physical alignment
